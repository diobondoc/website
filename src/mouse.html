Why do you need the best mouse?

As a gamer, the mouse is the key to winning your games. If the mouse isn't responsive enough or has high latency, you'll constantly be a step behind your opponents, and if it doesn't fit your hand comfortably or match your grip style, fatigue and discomfort can set in, hindering your performance during long gaming sessions.

Below are some of the best mice, categorized by budget, that you can use to win!

Budget Gaming Mice

Glorious Model O

For first-person shooter games, the GLORIOUS Model O is a great mouse. With a configurable polling rate, excellent DPI settings, and reduced click latency, it performs admirably. Additionally, it feels sturdy and ought to be cozy enough for extended gaming sessions. This one might be a little too big for people with small hands, so they might want to look at the GLORIOUS Model O- instead. Reference: RTINGS

  • Weight: 67 grams
  • Sensor: Pixart PMW-3360
  • Buttons: 6 programmable buttons
  • Pros: Low latency, light
  • Cons: Too big

Razer DeathAdder Essential

It boasts a short lift-off distance, a low minimum CPI, and a reasonable click latency, however some gamers could find it too slow. It feels sturdy and works with any kind of grip on practically all hand sizes, however small-handed folks could find it difficult to use a claw or fingertip grasp. Regretfully, its cable lacks flexibility and it is a little hefty. Reference: RTINGS

  • Weight: 105 grams
  • Sensor: 6,400 DPI Optical Sensor
  • Buttons: 5 programmable buttons
  • Pros: Good for all hand types, Well built
  • Cons: Heavy

Mid-range gaming mice

Logitech G502 X Plus

The Logitech G502 X Plus is the upgraded version of the G502 series, now equipped with LIGHTSPEED wireless technology and a HERO sensor for superior precision. It features a highly customizable design, including adjustable weights and 11 programmable buttons, perfect for gamers who need performance and flexibility. Reference: Tom's Hardware

  • Weight: 89 grams
  • Sensor: HERO 25K Optical Sensor
  • Buttons: 11 programmable buttons
  • Pros: LIGHTSPEED Wireless, Hero Sensor
  • Cons: None

SteelSeries Rival 600

The SteelSeries Rival 600 offers dual sensors for high-accuracy tracking, with a precision optical sensor and a dedicated depth sensor. It also features split-trigger buttons and customizable weight adjustments, making it one of the most adaptable mice for any gamer. Reference: IGN

  • Weight: Adjustable (up to 96 grams)
  • Sensor: TrueMove3+ Dual Sensor
  • Buttons: 7 programmable buttons
  • Pros: Dual sensor, Nice rgb lighting
  • Cons: Kind of expensive

High-End Gaming Mice

Razer Viper V3 Pro

The Razer Viper V3 Pro is an ultra-lightweight, ambidextrous mouse designed for esports professionals. With Razer's HyperSpeed wireless technology, the Viper V3 Pro offers unbeatable speed, precision, and comfort, making it one of the top choices for competitive gaming. Reference: TechRadar

  • Weight: 59 grams
  • Sensor: Razer Focus Pro 30K Optical Sensor
  • Buttons: 6 programmable buttons
  • Pros: Low sensor latency, Excellent build, 8000hz polling rate
  • Cons: None really

Logitech G Pro X Superlight

The Logitech G Pro X Superlight is an ultra-lightweight mouse designed for professional gamers. It offers superb accuracy with a HERO sensor and a weight of just 63 grams, making it perfect for quick, precise movements. Reference: PCWORLD

  • Weight: 63 grams
  • Sensor: HERO sensor
  • Buttons: 5 programmable buttons
  • Pros: HERO sensor, Looks good
  • Cons: lacks bluetooth, can be small to some
